SAWA CommandArtefacts redesign

Artefacts — 5 directions

Five takes on the new Artefacts section, all on the Minimal Light / Blue system. Same content model in every one — three categories (Outbound messages · LinkedIn Posts · Landing page) with a review / approval focus (surface what needs Adam, let him approve, editing secondary). They differ only in how the work is organised.

Option 1

Tabbed channel queues

A summary strip answers “what needs me?”, then a segmented control (Outbound / Posts / Landing) with a sliding active pill reveals each channel’s review queue — to-approve rows on top, approved/scheduled/live beneath.

Open mockup
Option 2

Unified approval inbox

One prioritised triage stream like an email client — everything awaiting Adam across all channels, soonest-to-ship first, channel-tagged. Two-pane: queue on the left, full preview + Approve on the right. Clear-your-inbox feel.

Open mockup
Option 3

Status board (kanban)

A horizontal board: Draft → Needs your review → Scheduled → Live. Channel-coded cards from all three categories flow left-to-right; the “Needs your review” column is emphasised in amber. Approve moves a card rightward.

Open mockup
Option 4

Campaign timeline

Three parallel lanes (Outbound / Posts / Landing) on a shared date axis from today to the Jul 21 dinner — so Adam sees when everything ships and what must be approved before its date. Today marker + days-to-dinner anchor.

Open mockup
Option 5

Stacked channel dashboard

One calm scroll. A hero states the job (“5 awaiting your approval”), then three stacked panels — Outbound, Posts, Landing — each surfacing to-approve items at the top and tucking scheduled/live work quietly beneath. Overview-first.

These are mockups, not wired-up pages. Buttons/animations are illustrative. Pick a direction (or mix two — e.g. a board’s structure with an inbox’s preview pane) and the next step is a short spec → implementation in the real Next.js app.
